Miso Soup

Description

Miso soup is a ubiquitous side dish found in most Japanese restaurants. It's even offered at sushi trains!

Miso soup's main ingredient, miso paste, is a fermented, salty, umami health food dating back hundreds of years to old Japan. Combining it with fish stock, wakame seaweed, tofu and optionally green onions makes a gut-healthy Japanese staple. Let's begin!

Ingredients

  • 4 cups water
  • 1 tablespoon hondashi no moto
  • 2 tablespoons miso paste (white or red is left to your preference)
  • 1 teaspoon dried wakame seaweed
  • 8 ounces of silken tofu
  • (optional) Finely chopped green onion

Steps

  1. Gather all your ingredients.
  2. Heat water to medium-low. Do not bring it to a boil.
  3. Stir in hondashi no moto and miso paste thorougly.
  4. Add wakame seaweed and tofu. Let wakame unfurl for a few minutes.
  5. Serve. Garnish with optional green onions.
